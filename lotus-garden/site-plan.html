The Lotus Garden is place to find uplifting messages to get you through rainy days by giving you motivating you to go out and act and remember the good when the world focuses on the negative. Let these parables, songs, scriptures, and more remind you of the light and provide you a source of inspiration.

The Most Idiotic Traveler in the World

There once was a traveler on a journey who kept getting tricked by people she came across. That is why everyone calls her an idiot. The villagers all scam something from her. They swindle her out of money and clothes, anything she has to give, but because the traveler is dumb, she doesn't understand what's really happening. So when the villagers lie and tell her she's saving them, she starts weeping. And she always tells them "I wish you peace and happiness" before she waves goodbye. Before long she has nothing left and is too weak to continue traveling. Before the last person to swindle something from her leaves, they say to the traveler, “Thank you. Here is a gift in return for your generosity.” But that, too, was a lie. The so-called gift is just a piece of paper with the word “idiot” written on it. Still the traveler weeps. “Thank you so much,” she says, “This is the first gift I've ever received. I'm so very happy, and I'm forever grateful to you. I can never repay you for your generosity.” Tears pour from her eyes, as the traveler passes away, dying in a pool of her joyful tears."

Most people will laugh at the travelor and call her an idiot, but I don't. I think this girl is a lovely person. It's a waste of time to worry about what you might lose in life, and so the travelor paid no mind to such things. How can we be a little more like the Idiotic Travelor who gave freely expecting nothing, and weeped with gratitude and happiness at the smallest gift? How much more good could we do in the world if we would not hesitate to give what we have to help another in need?

Music: "Have I Done Any Good", "Go and Do", "Embark"

Scriptures: D&C 6:13, D&C 56:16, Matthew 10:8

The Parable of the Chinese Farmer

"...an old Chinese farmer lost his best stallion one day and his neighbor came around to express his regrets, but the farmer just said, "Who knows what is good and what is bad." The next day the stallion returned bringing with him 3 wild mares. The neighbor rushed back to celebrate with the farmer, but the old farmer simply said, "Who knows what is good and what is bad." The following day, the farmer's son fell from one of the wild mares while trying to break her in and broke his arm and injured his leg. The neighbor came by to check on the son and give his condolences, but the old farmer just said, "Who knows what is good and what is bad." The next day the army came to the farm to conscript the farmer's son for the war, but found him invalid and left him with his father. The neighbor thought to himself, "Who knows what is good and what is bad."

Everytime you are faced with a situation, you have a choice: to view the glass half full or half empty, to trust God or trust your own understanding. If you try hard enough you can find something bad about every situation, but at the same time if you try hard enough you can find something good about every situation. We may not know why we are asked to face the situations presented to us, but we can choose to trust that God has our best interests at heart and will make everything works out in the end. "If it has not worked out yet, then it is not the end" (Tracy McMillion).

Music: "Good Day", "Silver Lining", "If You Believe"

Scriptures: D&C 98:3, Isaiah 5:20, Exodus 14:13
